# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA / Test Engineers

### Role Summary
QA / Test Engineers ensure quality is planned and validated throughout delivery, not only at release time.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain test strategy and quality gates
- Validate acceptance criteria and regression coverage
- Partner with delivery teams on defect triage and release readiness
- Track and report quality risks

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Keep quality signals visible across planning, execution, and release
- Improve confidence in release decisions

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and test execution updates
- Defect triage summaries
- Release readiness sign-off notes

### Collaboration Matrix
- Works most closely with: Developers, Project Managers, Product Managers

---

## UX/UI Designers

### Role Summary
UX/UI Designers shape user experience and accessibility so delivered solutions are usable and aligned with customer outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Translate requirements into user flows, wireframes, and design specs
- Validate usability and accessibility expectations
- Support implementation and design QA during execution
- Clarify design trade-offs during planning and release decisions

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce rework by aligning product/design/engineering early
- Maintain design consistency across increments

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and walkthroughs
- Annotated mocks or prototypes
- Scope and trade-off discussions with PM/PdM/engineering

### Collaboration Matrix
- Works most closely with: Product Managers, Developers, Project Managers

---

## DevOps / SRE Engineers

### Role Summary
DevOps / SRE Engineers ensure reliable CI/CD, safe deployments, and operational readiness.

### Responsibilities
- Maintain deployment pipelines and environment reliability
- Define observability and rollback readiness requirements
- Partner with teams on infrastructure and release safety checks
- Support incident response during deployment issues

### Goals
- Minimize deployment risk and recovery time
- Improve release predictability and operational stability
- Keep runbooks and automation current

### Typical Communication
- CI/CD and environment status updates
- Deployment readiness and rollback plans
- Incident summaries and follow-up actions

### Collaboration Matrix
- Works most closely with: Developers, QA / Test Engineers, Security Engineers, Project Managers

---

## Security Engineers

### Role Summary
Security Engineers guide secure design and implementation decisions across the lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Assess security risks and recommend mitigations
- Review security-sensitive changes and controls
- Advise on incident readiness and response requirements
- Escalate high-risk issues for go/no-go decisions

### Goals
- Reduce security vulnerabilities in delivered increments
- Integrate security checks into planning, execution, and release
- Improve security awareness and response readiness

### Typical Communication
- Threat/risk assessments
- Security review feedback
- Security gate outcomes and incident-response coordination

### Collaboration Matrix
- Works most closely with: Developers, DevOps / SRE Engineers, Project Managers

---

## Data Analysts / Analytics Engineers

### Role Summary
Data Analysts / Analytics Engineers ensure teams can measure outcomes and make evidence-based decisions.

### Responsibilities
- Define instrumentation and KPI measurement requirements
- Validate analytics implementation and data quality
- Provide post-release insights against success metrics
- Highlight gaps in measurement coverage

### Goals
- Ensure product and project decisions are data-informed
- Improve confidence in outcome reporting
- Accelerate learning loops after releases

### Typical Communication
- KPI definitions and measurement plans
- Dashboard updates and trend summaries
- Post-release insights and recommendations

### Collaboration Matrix
- Works most closely with: Product Managers, Developers, Project Managers

---

## Customer Support / Success Representatives

### Role Summary
Customer Support / Success Representatives bring customer impact and operational readiness into delivery planning.

### Responsibilities
- Provide customer pain-point and incident trend input
- Prepare support readiness, known-issue messaging, and escalations
- Validate customer-facing communications for releases
- Help triage and reproduce customer-reported issues

### Goals
- Reduce customer-facing disruption during releases
- Improve issue response speed and clarity
- Ensure teams include support context in prioritization

### Typical Communication
- Customer-impact summaries
- Release support readiness check-ins
- Issue reproduction details and feedback loops

### Collaboration Matrix
- Works most closely with: Product Managers, Project Managers, QA / Test Engineers, Developers

---

## Engineering Managers / Technical Leads

### Role Summary
Engineering Managers / Technical Leads align technical direction, capacity, and execution quality with delivery commitments.

### Responsibilities
- Guide architecture and technical implementation strategy
- Balance team capacity, staffing, and delivery risks
- Support scope and sequencing trade-off decisions
- Coach teams on delivery quality and execution discipline

### Goals
- Keep delivery plans technically feasible and sustainable
- Reduce avoidable delivery risk and rework
- Improve execution quality and team effectiveness

### Typical Communication
- Technical strategy updates
- Capacity and delivery feasibility discussions
- Risk and escalation reviews with PM/PdM

### Collaboration Matrix
- Works most closely with: Developers, Project Managers, Product Managers

---
